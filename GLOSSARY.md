# Glossary

Definitions for terms used across **Bitcoin for the Arts Research** publications. This is the canonical, repo-side version of the glossary published at [bitcoinforthearts.org/research/glossary](https://bitcoinforthearts.org/research/glossary).

When a term appears in a report, it is used in the sense defined here.

---

## Arts funding and cultural policy

**Arts and Cultural Production Satellite Account (ACPSA)** — A BEA/NEA statistical account measuring the economic value of arts and cultural production in the United States and individual states.

**Local arts agency** — A local public or nonprofit entity that supports artists, arts organizations, cultural planning, grants, public art, or community arts activity.

**Operating support** — Funding that supports general operations — staffing, rent, utilities, administration, organizational capacity — rather than a single restricted project.

**Project support** — Funding restricted to a specific project, production, exhibition, residency, or program.

**Regranting** — A funding structure in which one agency or organization receives funds and redistributes them to eligible artists, organizations, or local partners.

**State arts agency** — A state-level public agency that supports arts participation, grants, cultural policy, and statewide arts infrastructure. Tracked in aggregate by NASAA.

**Unrestricted grant** — A grant that gives the recipient flexibility to use funds for broad mission or operating needs, subject to the grant agreement.

## Public finance

**Appropriation** — A formal authorization of public funds by a legislature or governing body for a specific agency, program, purpose, or fiscal year.

**ARPA — American Rescue Plan Act** — Federal legislation signed in March 2021 that, among other things, distributed roughly $350B to state and local governments through the *State and Local Fiscal Recovery Funds*. Funds had to be obligated by end of 2024 and spent by end of 2026. The 2026 expiration is the subject of *The ARPA Cliff*.

**Balance sheet** — A financial statement showing assets, liabilities, and net assets or equity at a point in time.

**Fiscal year** — The 12-month accounting period an institution uses. For most U.S. states fiscal year XX runs July 1 (year XX−1) to June 30 (year XX); the federal government runs October 1 to September 30.

**Line-item appropriation** — A specific appropriation for a named project, recipient, or program, often separate from a base agency budget.

**Operating expense** — A recurring cost required to run an organization — staff, rent, insurance, software, utilities, administration.

**SLFRF — State and Local Fiscal Recovery Funds** — The ARPA program through which roughly $350B was made available to state, local, territorial, and Tribal governments. Tracked by the U.S. Treasury.

**Spending rule** — A policy that determines how much an endowment or reserve may distribute each year, commonly based on a percentage of trailing market value.

## Nonprofit finance and philanthropy

**501(c)(3)** — A U.S. Internal Revenue Code section defining tax-exempt charitable organizations. Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc. is a 501(c)(3) public charity (EIN 41-2642260).

**Donor-advised fund (DAF)** — A philanthropic vehicle that allows donors to contribute, take an immediate tax deduction, and recommend grants over time.

**Endowment** — A pool of assets intended to support an institution or purpose over a long horizon, usually through investment returns and a spending policy.

**Form 990** — The annual information return filed by most U.S. tax-exempt organizations with the IRS. Public.

**Foundation giving** — Grants made by private, community, corporate, or operating foundations. Aggregated by Candid (formerly Foundation Center).

**Individual giving** — Donations from individual donors. Often tracked alongside foundation and corporate giving in the *Giving USA* report.

**Net assets** — A nonprofit's equivalent of equity: total assets minus total liabilities. Reported with or without donor restrictions.

## Bitcoin and monetary terms

**Bitcoin** — The first decentralized, peer-to-peer monetary network with a fixed supply of 21 million units. Used throughout BFTA Research strictly as monetary technology.

**Borderless currency** — A unit of monetary value that can be transmitted, received, and held without depending on any one country's financial intermediation.

**BTCPay Server** — Open-source, self-hosted Bitcoin payment processor. BFTA uses BTCPay for Bitcoin donations and grant payouts.

**Debasement** — A reduction in the purchasing power of a currency unit, historically through metal-content reduction and currently through monetary expansion of fiat currencies.

**Endowment durability** — The capacity of an endowment to maintain real purchasing power over a long horizon, net of spending and inflation.

**HODL** — A common Bitcoin community term for long-term holding. BFTA's *HODL Vault* is the multisig endowment reserve.

**Lightning Network** — A Layer 2 protocol for fast, low-fee Bitcoin payments. Used for micro-grants and live event payouts.

**Multisig** — A custody scheme requiring multiple cryptographic signatures to authorize a transaction. The standard for institutional Bitcoin reserves.

**Sound money** — Money whose supply cannot be inflated at the discretion of an issuer. The economic property that distinguishes Bitcoin from fiat currencies. Used in BFTA Research as the technical descriptor of Bitcoin's monetary properties, not as an advocacy term.

**Treasury reserve / HODL Vault** — BFTA's permanent Bitcoin endowment reserve, held in multisig custody and not used for operations.

## Research and citation terms

**Institutional byline** — The convention by which a report carries an organizational author rather than an individual one. BFTA reports are bylined *Bitcoin for the Arts Research*.

**Last reviewed** — The date a human reviewer last checked every source citation in a report against its current primary source. Displayed in the report hero.

**Primary source** — Original documentation produced by the entity to which a claim refers: budgets, financial statements, audited statements, agency reports, statistical-agency series, legislation, IRS filings.

**Source hierarchy** — The five-tier ranking of evidence used by BFTA Research. See [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md).

## Out of scope (referenced, not endorsed)

These terms appear in BFTA Research **only as referenced phenomena** when documenting institutional behavior. They are not part of BFTA's research mandate or institutional practice. See [ETHOS.md](ETHOS.md).

**NFT — Non-fungible token** — A blockchain-tracked claim to a digital or physical item. Referenced where relevant to documenting fundraising or institutional behavior; not endorsed as institutional practice.

**Ordinals / Inscriptions / BRC-20 / Runes** — Non-monetary uses of Bitcoin block space. Referenced where relevant; not part of BFTA's mission.

**Altcoin / token / "crypto"** — Any cryptocurrency or token other than Bitcoin. Outside BFTA's monetary research scope.

**Web3 / DAO** — Token-based application or governance frameworks. Referenced where relevant; not part of BFTA's research or operational practice.
