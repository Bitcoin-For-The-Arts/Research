## Abstract

The narrative, primary-source–cited editorial framing of *The ARPA Cliff* report. Built around Boston's FY2027 budget cycle and Mayor Wu's proposed 27% cut to the Mayor's Office of Arts and Culture, the article traces how the American Rescue Plan Act quietly subsidized American city arts budgets for four years, why that money is expiring at the end of 2026, and what happens when dozens of mid-sized US cities find out their underlying arts capacity has been masked by federal stimulus. Concludes with the structural argument for non-state-dependent funding.
