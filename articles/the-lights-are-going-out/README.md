## Abstract

A national-scope editorial built around San Diego's April 2026 proposal to cut city arts funding from $13.8M to under $2M — an 86% reduction. Connects San Diego to the FY2026 federal NEA targeting, the projected 7.7% decline in state arts agency appropriations, and the structural fiat-monetary pressures that make these cuts inevitable. The editorial framing of the broader *State of Arts Funding 2026* thesis: the old model is dying and the case for non-state-dependent arts patronage is now operational, not theoretical.

- **86%** — San Diego's proposed FY2026 cut to city arts funding ($13.8M → under $2M)
