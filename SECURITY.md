# Security & sensitive-data policy

This repository is public, citable research. Almost everything in it is intended to be published. This document covers the narrow set of things that are not, and how to report concerns.

---

## Reporting a security or data concern

Please email `security@bitcoinforthearts.org` (or `hello@bitcoinforthearts.org` if the dedicated address is unavailable) with:

- A description of the concern
- The file, commit, issue, or pull request involved
- Any relevant primary source (e.g. an applicable privacy law, a takedown request, or a redaction of an exhibit you control)

We aim to acknowledge within two business days. Verified concerns are addressed promptly and, where appropriate, publicly.

Do not open public GitHub issues for sensitive disclosures. Use email.

---

## What this repository is, and is not

**Is:** public funding data, state and federal appropriations, agency reports, IRS Form 990 data, foundation grant rolls, statistical-agency series, primary-source-cited research, charts, datasets, and the corresponding narrative reports.

**Is not:** any operational, donor, beneficiary, custodial, or treasury detail that could compromise the organization, a donor, or a grantee.

The Bitcoin for the Arts, Inc. **HODL Vault** address is **not public** and is not stored in this repository. Aggregate reserve and grantmaking figures are published at [bitcoinforthearts.org/transparency](https://bitcoinforthearts.org/transparency). Operational security is documented in the operating organization's private channels, not here.

---

## Personally identifiable information (PII)

We do not publish:

- Personally identifiable information about individual donors below the public-recognition thresholds set out in BFTA's donor policies
- Personally identifiable information about individual grant applicants or grantees beyond what those grantees have agreed to make public
- Personally identifiable information about third parties (artists, administrators, employees of other institutions) unless that information already appears in a public primary source we are citing

If you believe PII has been published in error, email `security@bitcoinforthearts.org`. We will review and redact where appropriate.

---

## Copyright and primary-source material

We cite primary sources, we link to them, and we may reproduce short excerpts under standard fair-use practice for citation, criticism, and research. We do not redistribute copyrighted reports in full where the rights holder has not granted permission.

If you are a rights holder and believe material in this repository exceeds appropriate citation use, email `security@bitcoinforthearts.org` with the specific file or commit. We will respond promptly.

---

## Bitcoin operational security

Operational guidance for Bitcoin custody, key management, multisig, and BTCPay deployment lives in the operating organization's private documentation, not in this research repository. If you are an arts institution evaluating Bitcoin treasury practice and need help, contact `hello@bitcoinforthearts.org` and we can route the request appropriately.

The research portion of *Sound Money for the Arts* analyzes Bitcoin endowment durability and reserve economics at the institutional level. It is not, and is not intended as, custody or operational guidance.
