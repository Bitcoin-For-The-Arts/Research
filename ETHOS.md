# Research ethos — what we cover, what we do not

This document defines the scope and the boundary of **Bitcoin for the Arts Research.** Read it before contributing.

The boundary is not a marketing position. It is the basis of the trust we ask from journalists, foundation officers, academic researchers, donors, and the artists we exist to support.

---

## We exist to advance one mission

> *Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc. is the first 501(c)(3) nonprofit paying artists in Bitcoin.*
>
> *In the sovereign spirit of Bitcoin — uncensorable money for uncensorable minds — we ignite a self-sustaining global renaissance in art.*

Our research program exists to **document, analyze, and improve the funding and economic landscape of the arts** — globally, on a sound-money standard. We study how artists, art institutions, theaters, museums, dance and music organizations, residencies, and nonprofit cultural infrastructure are funded today, and how that funding can be made sovereign, sustainable, and global through Bitcoin.

That is the entire mandate. Everything in this repository serves it.

---

## We are Bitcoin-only

Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc. is a **Bitcoin-only** organization. All grants are paid in Bitcoin. All reserves are held in Bitcoin. All research about money, monetary policy, and reserve currency analyzes **Bitcoin as monetary technology**.

We do not publish content advocating for, promoting, or analyzing as institutional practice:

- Other cryptocurrencies, tokens, or altcoins
- "Web3" frameworks, DAO governance, or token-curated registries
- NFTs as a category
- Ordinals, Inscriptions, BRC-20, Runes, or any non-monetary use of Bitcoin block space
- Use of the Bitcoin network for purposes other than the transaction of monetary value

When any of these subjects appear in our research, they appear **only as referenced phenomena** — for example, when documenting how a particular arts institution behaved during a fundraising cycle, what a particular philanthropic trend looked like, or where a particular practice diverges from our position. **Referencing is not endorsement.** A citation in a BFTA report indicates the existence of a fact, not approval of it.

We do not support the Bitcoin network being used for spam, non-monetary inscriptions, or any production that competes with monetary settlement for block space. This position is permanent. It is not a strategy. It is the basis of the trust we ask for.

## What this means for contributors

When proposing or contributing research:

- **Yes:** the federal and state arts funding landscape, foundation giving, individual donor trends, the working-artist economy, real-earnings decay, endowment durability, Bitcoin reserve economics, public-finance pressures on local arts budgets, the ARPA cliff, the case for non-state-dependent funding, patronage history, the multiplier effect of arts spending, global ministries of culture, comparative arts policy.
- **Yes:** Lightning Network adoption, Bitcoin payment rails, BTCPay Server, custody models, multisig endowment design, and similar **monetary-payment** topics — these are part of how the arts will be funded.
- **No:** altcoin or "crypto" market analysis, NFT marketplaces, ordinal/inscription markets, token-fundraising for arts organizations, DAOs governing arts collectives, "web3 patronage" frameworks.
- **Reference, not endorsement:** documenting that a museum sold an NFT, that an artist used an ordinal, or that a foundation experimented with token grants is acceptable **when relevant to a sourced claim about the funding landscape**, and is paired with the citation discipline in [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md).

If you are not sure whether a topic belongs, open a [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/Bitcoin-For-The-Arts/Research/discussions) or email `info@bitcoinforthearts.org`. We will help you scope it.

## Our credibility standard

Our credibility is the asset. Every word in this repository is written against the possibility that a foundation officer, journalist, academic researcher, or auditor is reading it.

- **Verifiable evidence only.** If we do not have a primary source, we do not publish the data. See [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md).
- **No hype.** Reports avoid advocacy language and partisan framing. The numbers speak.
- **AI assists, does not author.** AI tools may help gather, summarize, and draft. AI output is never treated as evidence. Every factual claim is traced to a reviewed primary source before publication.
- **AI imagery only depicts verified data.** AI-generated images may illustrate hero art, section headers, and chart explainers for **already-verified** data. AI imagery is never used to imply data we have not independently verified, and is labeled where used.
- **Institutional byline.** Reports carry the byline *Bitcoin for the Arts Research*. The byline is the standard, not an individual.

## The reference frame: ACS-grade rigor, on a global scale

Our reference institutions for what credibility looks like in nonprofit research:

- **American Cancer Society — *Cancer Facts & Figures*** — for the annual, institutional, citation-dense, downloadable-PDF reference document we aspire to be for the arts.
- **Brookings Institution — policy briefs** — for the seriousness of language, dense source apparatus, and policy framing.
- **Pew Research** — for data-first reports, transparent methodology, and visual restraint.
- **NEA Office of Research & Analysis** — for the sector-specific evidence standard we hold ourselves to.
- **National Assembly of State Arts Agencies — annual state appropriations reports** — for ground-truth state-by-state public funding data.

We are a **borderless currency for a borderless community.** US-headquartered today, global mandate from day one. Our research must hold up under scrutiny in any country where someone reads it.

---

## What we will never do

- Publish a statistic we cannot trace to a primary source
- Promote a non-Bitcoin token, NFT, or ordinal product
- Frame Bitcoin as anything other than monetary technology
- Quietly rewrite history — corrections are dated, sourced, and preserved in git history
- Take a grant, sponsorship, or partnership that requires us to soften the research standard or the Bitcoin-only ethos

These are not aspirations. They are constraints we have already accepted.

---

## A note on language

Our writing aims for the register of a federal statistical agency or a major foundation's research arm. Plain. Precise. Footnoted. Confident without being loud. The audience is the journalist deciding whether to cite us, the program officer deciding whether to grant to us, the academic deciding whether to assign us, and the artist deciding whether to trust us. We write for them.
