## Abstract

A research brief on how temporary American Rescue Plan arts relief moved through federal, state, and local systems — and what happens as those dollars run off at the end of 2026. Built from primary sources at the National Endowment for the Arts, the U.S. Treasury, the National League of Cities, the National Assembly of State Arts Agencies, Arts Midwest, and individual state and municipal recovery programs. The case study throughout is Boston's FY2027 budget cycle and Mayor Wu's proposed 27% cut to the Mayor's Office of Arts and Culture.
