## Abstract

BFTA's flagship annual report on public, private, and non-state-dependent funding of the arts in the United States, with an opening framing for the global research mandate ahead. The report establishes the funding landscape, documents the collapse of 2024–2026, traces who is hurt first, summarizes the market response from foundations and individual donors, and concludes with the analytical case for non-state-dependent funding.

## Expected sections

1. Executive summary — four hero statistics, charted
2. The funding landscape today — federal, state, local, private
3. The collapse — 2024–2026 timeline of cuts, ARPA cliff, NEA targeting
4. Who gets hurt — artist demographics, geographic distribution
5. The market response — foundation giving and individual donor trends
6. The case for non-state-dependent funding — BFTA's thesis as analytical conclusion
7. References and methodology
8. Downloadable PDF
