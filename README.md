## What this repository is

This is the public, citable, contributable home of **Bitcoin for the Arts Research** — the research arm of [Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc.](https://bitcoinforthearts.org), the first 501(c)(3) nonprofit paying artists in Bitcoin.

Our mandate is to build a **doctoral-grade, primary-source research program on the funding and economic landscape of the arts — globally, on a sound-money standard.** We model our standard of evidence on the most respected nonprofit research programs in the world: the American Cancer Society's *Cancer Facts & Figures*, the Brookings Institution's policy briefs, Pew Research's data reports, and the National Endowment for the Arts' *Research & Analysis* series.

Every claim in this repository is **traceable to a primary source.** If we do not have a reference, we do not publish the data. Period.

> **Institutional byline.** All work in this repository is published under "Bitcoin for the Arts Research." There are no personal bylines on reports. The byline is a standard, not a person.

## What we study

The economic, fiscal, demographic, and institutional reality of how the arts are funded — and how they could be funded better — anywhere in the world.

| Domain | Examples of what we research |
|---|---|
| **Public funding of the arts** | NEA, state arts agencies, local arts agencies, line-item appropriations, ARPA / SLFRF, international ministries of culture |
| **Private funding of the arts** | Foundation giving (Candid / Foundation Center), corporate giving, individual donor trends, donor-advised funds |
| **The working-artist economy** | Real earnings, gig income, healthcare, housing, geographic distribution, demographic equity |
| **Art institutions & nonprofits** | Theaters, museums, dance and music organizations, residencies, regional cultural ecosystems, balance-sheet health |
| **Endowments and reserves** | Endowment durability, spending rules, the limits of fiat endowments under monetary debasement |
| **Sound money for the arts** | Bitcoin reserve durability, purchasing-power decay, debasement's effect on grants and operating budgets |
| **Patronage history** | From Renaissance commissions through the modern grant economy to Bitcoin-native patronage |

## The research standard

Read this in full before contributing: **[METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md)**.

The short version:

1. **Primary sources first.** Budgets, appropriations bills, audited financial statements, agency reports, and statistical-agency series outrank summaries, commentary, and social media.
2. **No unsourced statistics.** A number without a primary source is not ready for publication. Estimates must be labeled as such with their methodology disclosed.
3. **No hype.** Reports avoid advocacy language, partisan framing, and inflated claims. The evidence carries the argument.
4. **Numbers are taken seriously.** Budgets, costs, balance sheets, grant rolls, and revenue lines are among the strongest guides to institutional reality, and we treat them that way.
5. **Limitations are explicit.** Preliminary, proposed, lagged, revised, or estimated data is labeled. We disclose what we do not know.
6. **Institutional byline.** Reports are published under "Bitcoin for the Arts Research." The byline is the standard, not the author.

### Source hierarchy

| Tier | Source |
|---|---|
| 1 | Budgets, financial statements, audited statements, grant guidelines, official operating documents |
| 2 | Government and statistical agencies (BEA, BLS, Treasury, NEA, NASAA, state and municipal finance offices, international equivalents) |
| 3 | Peer-reviewed and university research with transparent methods |
| 4 | Major nonprofit research organizations (Brookings, Pew, Candid / Foundation Center, Americans for the Arts) |
| 5 | Reputable journalism and institutional reporting for live case studies and local examples when primary documents are unavailable |

### AI policy

AI tools may assist with searching, summarizing, outlining, and drafting. **AI output is not evidence.** Every factual claim in every report must be traceable to a reviewed primary source before publication. Claims generated by AI without source verification are excluded or held for review.

We may use AI-generated imagery to **depict already-verified data** in hero art, chart explainers, and section headers. AI imagery is never used to create the data itself, and any AI-generated image is labeled.

## What we cover — and what we do not

Read this in full: **[ETHOS.md](ETHOS.md)**.

### We are Bitcoin-only.

Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc. is a **Bitcoin-only** organization. All grants are paid in Bitcoin. All reserves are held in Bitcoin. All research about money, monetary policy, and reserve currency analyzes **Bitcoin as monetary technology** — sound money for institutions, artists, and endowments.

We do not publish content advocating for, promoting, or analyzing as institutional practice:

- Other cryptocurrencies, tokens, or altcoins
- NFTs as a category
- Ordinals, Inscriptions, BRC-20, Runes, or any non-monetary use of Bitcoin block space
- "Web3" frameworks, DAOs, or token-based governance
- Use of the Bitcoin network for purposes other than the transaction of monetary value

When these subjects appear in our research, they appear **only as referenced phenomena** — for example, when documenting how a particular institution behaved, what a particular fundraising trend looked like, or why a particular practice diverges from our position. Referencing is not endorsement. Our mission and ethos are unchanged: we support Bitcoin as monetary technology and we do not support the Bitcoin network being used for spam or any other non-monetary production.

If you are reading this as a journalist, foundation officer, or donor: this position is permanent, not strategic. It is the basis of the trust we ask for.

## How to contribute

Read this in full: **[CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)**.

We welcome contributors at every level:

- **Researchers and economists** — propose a new report, contribute a data appendix, peer-review an existing draft
- **Arts administrators and program officers** — share budget documents, grant rolls, appropriation records (public information helps everyone)
- **Public-finance and nonprofit-finance specialists** — strengthen our methodology, audit our chart data, file corrections
- **Data volunteers and developers** — help us build cleaner datasets, map state and municipal arts spending, or build tools that read our JSON datasets
- **Translators** — help us extend our research beyond English so global creators and global funders can use it
- **Writers and editors** — help us tighten language, eliminate hype, and keep the standard high

### Submit a correction or source

Open a [GitHub issue](https://github.com/Bitcoin-For-The-Arts/Research/issues) or email `info@bitcoinforthearts.org` with:

1. The report title and section
2. The exact claim or data point in question
3. The source you are referencing (with page, table, or line number when applicable)
4. Any context that helps us evaluate the change

Corrections are public. When we revise a report, the prior version is preserved in git history and the "last reviewed" date on the live report is updated.

## Why we do this

The funding landscape for the arts is changing faster than most people realize. ARPA recovery dollars expire in 2026. State arts appropriations entered FY2026 below FY2025. Municipal property-tax bases are under structural pressure. Foundation giving is consolidating. The NEA has been targeted in successive federal budget cycles.

Working artists, art institutions, theaters, dance and music organizations, residencies, museums, and the nonprofits that support them face a **funding cliff that is not theoretical** — it is happening in fiscal years currently being adopted.

At the same time, the world has a new monetary technology that no institution or inflation can confiscate. Bitcoin is sound money, borderless, and 24/7. We believe — and we are building the evidence to support — that a Bitcoin-native funding standard can stabilize, sustain, and ultimately expand the global arts economy in ways no fiat endowment, no national arts council, and no philanthropic intermediary has ever been able to.

Bitcoin For The Arts, Inc. is US-headquartered today. **Our research mandate is global from day one.** Bitcoin is a borderless currency; the global arts community is a borderless community; the research that supports them must be built to that same standard.

This is the work.
